Therapea

An all-in-one app to help people with eating disorders

Winter 2021

Project Type: Product Mockup

Role: Designer, Concept, Prototype

Tools: Figma

How might we simplify providing access to treatment and coping resources for people with eating disorders during the pandemic?

Concept Overview

Therapea is an all-in-one platform that has the features of providing professional, community, and educational support. To provide access to treatment, we implemented a booking system for appointments with healthcare professionals as well as being able to those meetings. The education resource center ensures credible posts that are specifically tailored to those with eating disorders and differ from the generic content one might find online. The social platform within the app fosters a community where users can talk to people with similar experiences and validate each others' emotions and struggles. Having these three main features, Therapea aims to provide our users with different aspects of support conveniently on their phones.

Problem Space

According to a July survey by the International Journal of Eating Disorders, 62% of people in the US with eating disorders like anorexia experienced a worsening of symptoms as the pandemic hit. As we're living through the COVID-19 pandemic, we believe this is an important topic to address as these kinds of mental health issues are worsened during a pandemic. Thus, we thought of helping people with eating disorders in this on-going pandemic. Our aim is to create a solution that provides access to coping resources and treatment for people with eating disorders in a pandemic. With our project, we are looking to create more holistic access to resources in one platform by providing treatment options, education and coping resources, and a support system to help those with eating disorders during the pandemic.

Brainstorm

To tackle this issue, our team came up with sketches of possible ideas on how to help people with eating disorders. We had ideas of making a zine, a newsletter, or a physical product. In the end, we decided to roll with working with an mobile phone application since that would be the easiest way for us to reach our main users. If help can be right on their phones, it would make for a convenient and socially distanced way of help.

Design Principles

  • Professional Support: Users of our app can pick which therapist they want and sign up for virtual sessions. Through the video function, they can meet with their therapists without having to leave their houses.
  • Community: In our app, we plan to incorporate a chat system where users can talk to others who have also experienced eating disorders. In addition, a video function for users to have meals with one another virtually.
  • Education: Users of our app will have access to a forum board to ask and answer questions anonymously. Articles and videos posted are crafted by professionals themselves.

Low Fidelity Prototype

We first started with a low fidelity prototype to visualize the UI and get an idea of what we want for the concept.

Usability Testing

Our team went through series of usability testing and research as we built our prototype. With some research and testing, we found multiple areas of improvements as well as some suggestions from our test subjects.

More formulated survey => Adding options for survey

Better visibility => Color contrast and emphasis

Safety issues => Report and block options on friend profiles

Final Product

In response to our research and what our users had said, we aimed for an all-in-one app that has better affordance and understanding of our users’ needs. In our high fidelity prototype, we incorporated five main interfaces: On boarding (login/signup, introduction, and survey), appointment booking, education resource center, friends+chat, and a main dashboard.

Key Takeaways

  • My first ever prototype! It was really fun being able to build a solution through design
  • Design is an iterative process, it takes time and patience
  • Interview and user test with not only your direct stakeholders, but also indirect stakeholders to gain a deeper understanding of the problem space and how your product affects them
  • When collaborating with other designers, make sure everyone's thoughts are taken into consideration and find a way to reach a consensus or make compromises
